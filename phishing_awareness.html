🏠 What is Phishing?

Definition

Phishing is a cyberattack where attackers impersonate trusted entities — banks, colleagues, or services — to steal credentials, financial data, or install malware. It is the #1 entry point for data breaches worldwide.

📊 By the Numbers

91% of cyberattacks start with phishing 3.4 billion phishing emails sent daily $17,700 lost per minute to phishing 65% increase in phishing since 2019

🎯 Types of Phishing

  • Email Phishing — Mass emails pretending to be legit companies
  • Spear Phishing — Targeted attack on a specific individual
  • Whaling — Targeting executives (CEO, CFO)
  • Smishing — Phishing via SMS text messages
  • Vishing — Phishing over phone calls
  • Clone Phishing — Duplicating a real email with a malicious link

📧 Recognizing Phishing Emails

⚠️ Example: Fake Bank Alert

From:security@paypa1-alerts.com
To:you@email.com
Subject:URGENT: Your account has been suspended!
Dear Valued Customer,

We have detected suspicious activity on your account. Your account has been temporarily suspended. You must verify your identity within 24 hours or your account will be permanently closed.

Click here to verify: http://paypa1-secure-login.xyz/verify

PayPal Security Team

Red flags found in this email ↓

Spoofed Domain "paypa1-alerts.com" — note the number 1 instead of letter L
Urgency "24 hours or permanently closed" — pressure tactics
Suspicious URL "paypa1-secure-login.xyz" — not paypal.com
Generic greeting "Valued Customer" instead of your name

🔍 The 7 Red Flags

  • Sender email doesn't match the company's real domain
  • Urgent or threatening language
  • Suspicious or mismatched links (hover before clicking!)
  • Unexpected attachments (.exe, .zip, .doc with macros)
  • Grammar and spelling errors
  • Requests for passwords, card numbers, or personal info
  • Too-good-to-be-true offers or prize notifications

🌐 Identifying Fake Websites

URL Comparison

✅ Legitimate: https://www.paypal.com/signin
❌ Fake: http://paypa1-secure.xyz/signin
❌ Fake: https://paypal.com.phishing-site.ru/signin
❌ Fake: https://secure-paypal-login.net
🔒

Check HTTPS

Lock icon ≠ safe. Phishing sites also use SSL. Check the actual domain.

🔍

Inspect URLs

Hover over links. Look for typosquatting: amaz0n, g00gle, paypa1.

🏠

Real Domain Rule

The real domain is always right before .com/.org. Anything else is a subdomain trick.

📋

WHOIS Lookup

Fake sites are often registered recently (days/weeks). Check whois.domaintools.com

🎨

Poor Design

Blurry logos, broken images, mismatched fonts are signs of a cloned site.

📞

Verify Directly

If unsure, call the company directly or type the URL manually.

🧠 Social Engineering Tactics

Pretexting

Attacker creates a fabricated scenario — pretending to be IT support, a bank auditor, or a vendor — to gain trust and extract information.

"Hi, this is Mike from IT. We noticed unusual activity on your account. I need your username and password to fix it."

Baiting

Leaving infected USB drives in public places or offering free software downloads that contain malware. Curiosity becomes the vulnerability.

Quid Pro Quo

Offering something of value in exchange for information — e.g., "free tech support" in exchange for your login credentials.

Tailgating / Piggybacking

Physically following an authorized person into a restricted area — not digital, but used in combined attacks.

Authority & Fear Triggers

Attackers impersonate authority figures (CEO, IRS, police) and create fear to bypass rational thinking. Always verify independently.

🛡 Best Practices to Stay Safe

🔐

Use MFA

Multi-factor authentication stops attackers even if your password is stolen.

🔑

Password Manager

Use unique strong passwords for every account. Never reuse passwords.

🚫

Don't Click

Never click links in unexpected emails. Go directly to the website instead.

📎

No Attachments

Don't open attachments from unknown senders. Scan with antivirus first.

🔄

Keep Updated

Update OS, browser, and apps regularly. Patches fix exploited vulnerabilities.

🎓

Stay Educated

Phishing evolves. Attend regular security awareness training.

📢

Report It

Report suspicious emails to your IT team or forward to phishing@your-org.com

🧪

Test Yourself

Use phishing simulation tools like GoPhish to test your organization.

🌐

VPN

Use a trusted VPN on public Wi-Fi to prevent man-in-the-middle attacks.

🚨 What To Do If You Clicked

  • Disconnect from the internet immediately
  • Change your passwords from a different device
  • Notify your IT/security team
  • Run a full antivirus scan
  • Monitor accounts for suspicious activity

📝 Knowledge Quiz

Test what you've learned. Select the best answer for each question.

Q1: You receive an email from "security@amaz0n-support.net" asking you to verify your account. What is the first red flag?

The domain "amaz0n-support.net" is not Amazon's real domain (amazon.com). Typosquatting replaces letters with similar-looking characters.

Q2: A website shows a padlock (🔒) and uses HTTPS. This means the site is:

HTTPS only means the connection is encrypted — it doesn't verify the site's legitimacy. Phishing sites routinely obtain SSL certificates.

Q3: What is "spear phishing"?

Spear phishing is highly targeted — attackers research the victim and personalize the attack, making it far more convincing than generic phishing.

Q4: You accidentally clicked a phishing link. What should you do FIRST?

Disconnecting immediately prevents malware from communicating with command-and-control servers. Then notify IT and change your passwords.

Q5: Which of these URLs is MOST likely legitimate for logging into PayPal?

Only option D uses PayPal's real domain (paypal.com) directly before the TLD (.com). In all other options, the actual domain is something else.
